The Concept

What is (De)BuggyBoard?

An interactive, educational breadboard
to ease circuit-building and debugging.

(De)BuggyBoard is an interactive, educational breadboard comprising of hardware and software components. Students design the circuit they want to build on a user interface, then as they build it on the attached breadboard, their circuit design will sync with the software to provide real-time information about their circuit.

The Motivation

The future of introductory circuits education.

This is (De)Buggy Board.

With traditional breadboards, there is a lot of cutting wires, creating a mess of connections, and tediously testing each component with a multimeter when things go wrong.

With the (De)Buggy Board, the student will be able to see what voltages are at each node, current and resistance information, and perhaps most importantly, notice when something goes awry.

The Competitive Analysis

Build18 Projects

There have been two similar projects within Carnegie Mellon’s annual Build18 hardware hackathon.

The ProtoSmart team created a breadboard that sensed voltages at each row of a small breadboard. However, that design was limited by the on-chip analog to digital converters of the microcontroller.

The Field Programmable Protoboard team set out to make a breadboard that wires rows together for you (auto-routing). However, the sheer size of the routing became to complex to accomplish for them with transistors and shift registers.

Egyptian Team

An eight-person team from Egypt designed an auto routing breadboard that uses a software backend to route nodes. Their design and goals are similar to ours, but lack the real-time voltage tracking that is essential for our debugging application.

No Wires Breadboard

This the only product for sale similar to our concept that we could find.

It has features similar to ours, such as an attached software to plan your breadboard wiring, and LEDs to guide placement.

The Requirements

Functional

Hardware

Nodal Voltages

Measure all nodal voltages on breadboard and relay voltage data to software.

Emergency Shutdown

Detect dangerous user circuit errors (i.e., shorts) and power off the board to prevent hardware damage.

Status LED's

Indicate the current status of each pin row on the breadboard via a green, yellow, or red LED.

Software

Circuit Diagramming

Provide a user interface for symbolically diagramming circuits. Automagically perform basic error checks on the circuit diagram via KCL and KVL.

Placing and Wiring Components

Create an interface for placing and wiring circuit components from the circuit diagram on on virtual breadboard.

Debugging Feedback

Provide real-time debugging feedback derived from nodal voltages. Is my resistor disconnected? Did I place the capacitor in the correct pins?


Non-Functional

Safety

The circuit should detect dangerously invalid circuits (such as short circuits and high currents) and avoid unsafe operating conditions by shutting down. As first priority, the circuit should not pose physical harm to users through unsafe operation. Second, the circuit’s safety system should prevent internal damage.

Reliability

We will have reliable and accurate nodal voltage readings for students to successfully understand the physical circuit.

The Components

The crucial parts of our device.

  • Crosspoint Switch

    For the auto-routing feature and current sensing capabilities.

  • Microcontroller Unit

    We need only a simple MCU, will use an Atmel.

  • Analog to Digital Converter

    For voltage sensing, we need high quality ADCs.

  • Analog Mux

    For utilizing one sensor component across multiple channels.

  • RGB LEDs

    For lighting up rows of the breadboard for feedback.

  • Small Breadboard

    The all-important breadboard.

  • Shift Register

    To send information down the LEDs in a simple manner.
